Warm-Up — ~10 min

Work through in order. Single-leg work later asks for balance and right-side control — prime the ankle, hip and glute well.

01
Ankle circles — 10 each direction, each foot. First, given the Achilles history. Extra time if the right heel feels stiff.
02
Glute bridges — 2 × 10. Drive hips up, squeeze hard at the top, lower slowly. Wakes the posterior chain.
03
Hip circles / single-leg balance — 10 per leg, or 30 s balance per leg. Opens the hip; rehearses single-leg stance.
04
Bodyweight squats — 10 slow, parallel or below. Rehearses the knee-extension pattern the Leg Press loads.
05
Leg swings (front-back) — 10 per leg. Loosen hamstrings and hip flexors.
06
Calf raises (bodyweight) — 2 × 8 slow, 3 s down. Prep for the calf work; extra 30 s if the right Achilles feels tight.
07
Dead bug (unloaded) — 5 per side. Feel the lower back press flat into the floor.
Exercises
01 / 06
Leg Press
Life Fitness Optima · OSLP · 3 × 8 @ 44 kg · Rest 90–120 sec
Seated horizontal press — quads first, glutes and hamstrings assisting, no spinal load.
Quick reset
  • Back flush; knees ~90° at start; whole foot on the platform; latches engaged.
Tap when done
Key cues
  • Push the floor away — glutes pinned to the seat, don't lift the hips.
  • Knees track over the toes — don't let the right knee cave inward under fatigue. That's your old collapse pattern; the late reps are where it shows.
  • Soft lockout, 2 s back.
44 kg is the working target. If set 1 sits at RPE ≤5, hold today and we step up next session; if form breaks or the right knee caves, drop a pin. Log the weight + RPE.
02 / 06
Romanian Deadlift
Dumbbells · 3 × 8 @ 10 kg/hand · Rest 90 sec
Hip-hinge loading the hamstrings and glutes eccentrically. Strong hamstrings offload the calf and Achilles — one of the most tendon-protective lifts here.
Quick reset
  • Dumbbell each hand at the hips; soft knees.
Tap when done
Key cues
  • Hips back, not down — if the knees bend a lot, you're squatting.
  • Long spine — neutral back, don't round the upper back.
  • 2–3 s down — the slow eccentric is the point.
The RDL lengthens the calf complex under load. Slow, controlled descent; a muscle stretch is fine, anything sharp in the tendon is a stop signal — log it.
03 / 06
Bulgarian Split Squat
Dumbbells · 3 × 8/leg @ 6 kg/hand · Rest 60–90 sec
Single-leg squat with the rear foot elevated — the most running-specific lift here, with high demand on hip stability and glute strength.
Quick reset
  • Rear foot on the bench; front shin vertical at the bottom; DBs at the sides.
Tap when done
Key cues
  • Front knee tracks the toe — the right side especially.
  • Drive through the front heel — more glute than pushing through the toe.
  • Tall torso, 2 s down.
▶ New variation today
04 / 06
Calf Raise — Slow Eccentric
Bodyweight · 3 × 15 BW · Rest 60 sec
Direct calf and Achilles loading; the slow 3-second lowering is where the tendon benefit comes from. Your primary Achilles-resilience work — these reps count as today's daily heel drops.
Machine setup
Step edge if available (heel drops below the edge for full range); flat ground is fine.
Fingertips on a wall for balance only — no weight through the hand.
Tap when done
Instructions
  1. Rise onto the balls of the feet — push through the big toe, don't roll to the outer edge. Pause 1 s.
  2. Lower slowly — count 3 seconds — feeling the calf lengthen under load.
  3. Full stretch at the bottom before the next rep.
Key cues
  • Three seconds down — count it; fast reps miss the point.
  • Push through the big toe — ball-of-foot loading, the pattern your rehab targets.
  • Full range — as high up, as low down as you can.
Single-leg roughly doubles the load per tendon. Calf-belly burn is fine; anything sharp in the tendon (the cord above the heel) is a stop signal — drop that set to double-leg and log it. Note any left/right difference.
05 / 06
Copenhagen Plank
Bodyweight · 3 × 35 s/side BW · Rest 90 sec
Side-plank loading the hip adductors under a long lever — one of the best-evidenced groin-stability exercises for runners, and direct carry-over from your 2021 rehab.
Quick reset
  • Forearm down, top foot/ankle on a bench; knee version if the hold gets sloppy.
Tap when done
Key cues
  • Push the surface away — the active drive is what loads the adductor.
  • Stack the hips — squared hips keep the right side out of its collapse pattern.
  • Quality over the clock — a clean shorter hold beats a sagging long one.
Sharp groin/adductor pain or a notably weaker right side — shorten the hold or use the knee version, and log it.
06 / 06
Dead Bug
Bodyweight · 3 × 12 BW · Rest 60 sec
Anti-extension core — the trunk holds steady while the limbs drive, the same job it does each stride.
Quick reset
  • On your back, tabletop legs, lower back pressed flat throughout.
Tap when done
Key cues
  • Back flat like a table.
  • Slow — 3 s out, 2 s back, opposite arm and leg.
Cool-Down — ~8–10 min

Calf and Achilles first — you've loaded the knee/hinge complex hard.

01
Calf + soleus stretch (wall) — 45 s straight-knee then 30 s bent-knee per leg. Both sides.
02
Standing quad stretch — 45 s per leg.
03
Hamstring stretch — 45 s per leg.
04
Half-kneeling hip-flexor stretch — 45–90 s per side. Tuck the pelvis first, then drive forward. Today's daily hip-flexor work — ticked off.
05
Figure-4 / pigeon — 60 s per side. Opens the hip rotators.
06
Child's pose — 60 s. Finish here.
