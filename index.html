Daily routines — full instructions

›

The Today panel tells you which of these apply each day; this section is the how. The doubling-up is resolved by one rule: each modality once per day — whichever session includes it ticks it off. Wednesday's slow-eccentric calf raises count as that day's heel drops; any post-run hip-flexor stretch counts as that day's daily one.

Eccentric heel drops — daily Achilles work ›

Highest-leverage daily intervention for the Achilles. Do every day — except Wednesday lower-strength days, where the session's slow-eccentric calf raises are the day's dose. Evening is best; if morning, leave ≥30 min before running.

ⓘSetup: forefoot on a step edge, heel hanging off. Hold a wall for balance. Do both variations every session — straight-leg first, then bent-knee.
Straight-leg (first)Knee fully extended — loads the gastrocnemius, the large superficial calf muscle.
Bent-knee 20–30° (second)Knee slightly bent — loads the soleus, the deeper calf. Critical for insertional issues.

The movement

  1. Rise onto tiptoes on both feet — about 1.5 s up
  2. Shift weight entirely to the right leg
  3. Lower the heel slowly over 3–4 s — below step level when able
  4. A deep ache through the calf at the bottom is expected and fine
  5. Rise back up on both feet — never push up one-legged from the bottom
  6. All straight-leg reps first, then the full set bent-knee

⚠Stop signal: pain escalating within a set, or next-morning baseline worse than the day before → drop back to double-leg for a few days.
△Which type? Pinch the tendon upward from the heel bone. Tender at the heel bone = insertional — stay at flat-only until symptom-free 2+ weeks. Tender 3–5 cm up the tendon = mid-portion — progress below-neutral as scheduled.
Hip flexor & couch stretch ›

Half-kneeling hip flexor — daily (post-run version counts)

Most important daily stretch for a desk-sitting runner. Tight hip flexors alter gait and shift load down the chain toward the Achilles. On run days, doing it in the post-run stretches ticks it off — don't repeat it.

  1. Kneel on the right knee, left foot forward — left knee ~90°, foot flat
  2. Critical first: squeeze the right glute and tuck the pelvis slightly under. Skip this and you'll arch the lower back instead of stretching the target.
  3. Trunk tall, gently drive hips forward over the fixed knee
  4. Feel it deep at the front of the right hip and groin — not the lower back
  5. Hold 45–90 s — it deepens around the 20–30 s mark
  6. Swap sides
✓PNF option (more effective): from the stretch, pull the rear knee forward into the floor for 5 s (isometric — nothing moves), relax, let it deepen. Repeat 2–3× per side.

Couch stretch — 3–4× per week

  1. Face away from a wall; right shin vertical up the wall, knee near the floor, top of foot on the wall
  2. Left foot forward into a lunge, flat on the floor
  3. Trunk tall and vertical — leaning forward kills the stretch
  4. Start the knee 10–15 cm from the wall; work closer over weeks
  5. Deep stretch in the front of the right thigh and hip
  6. Hold 60–90 s per side
△If the foot position bothers the Achilles, angle the foot slightly out of full plantarflexion.
Before every run — dynamic warm-up (~3 min) ›

All four movements, then straight into the easy warm-up jog — the jog is the cardiovascular warm-up; nothing extra needed.

  1. Ankle circles — slow, deliberate circles at the ankle only, ~3–4 s per circle. 10 each direction, each foot.
  2. Leg swings, front-to-back — side-on to a wall, pendulum swing to ~waist height. Range grows naturally. 10–15 per leg.
  3. Leg swings, side-to-side — facing the wall, across the body then out. 10–15 per leg.
  4. Pre-run calf raises — flat ground (no step), 1.5 s up / 2 s down, no bounce. 10–15 reps. This is smooth activation, not the therapeutic heel drops — both stay.
After every run — static stretches (~8–10 min) ›

The easy cooldown jog handles the cardiovascular side; these target warm muscle and tendon.

  1. Straight-leg calf stretch — hands on wall, rear leg straight, heel flat, lean through the hips. 30–45 s per side.
  2. Bent-knee soleus stretch — same position, rear knee bent 20–30°, heel down. The more important of the two for the Achilles — don't skip. 30–45 s per side.
  3. Hip flexor — half-kneeling version (see above). Counts as today's daily hip-flexor work. 45–90 s per side.
  4. Hamstring — seated, one leg long, hinge from the hips with a long spine. 30–45 s per side.
  5. Foam roll calves (optional) — 60–90 s per calf, mainly as a diagnostic: a right calf consistently tighter than the left is an early Achilles warning.
Rest days — activation & mobility (~15 min) ›

On top of the daily heel drops and hip-flexor work. Under-activated glutes push compensatory stress down to the lower leg.

1 — Glute bridges · 3 × 15–20

  1. On your back, knees ~90°, feet flat hip-width — close enough to almost touch your heels
  2. Squeeze the glutes before lifting — the step most people skip
  3. Drive hips to a straight line knees→shoulders; no lower-back hyperextension
  4. Hold 1–2 s at the top, actively squeezing
  5. Lower over 2–3 s
ⓘProgression: single-leg bridge when double-leg is easy — much harder and reveals left-right asymmetry.

2 — Clamshells · 2–3 × 15–20 per side

  1. On your side, hips stacked directly over each other — critical
  2. Knees stacked, bent 45–60°, feet together
  3. Pelvis completely still, rotate the top knee toward the ceiling — 2 s up, 2 s down
  4. Shrink the range before you let the pelvis rotate — the most common fault
⚠Wrong places to feel it: the very side of the hip (TFL taking over) or the inner thigh of the bottom leg. Right place: side/rear of the top hip.

3 — Foam roll, full body · ~8 min

60–90 s per area: calves (priority — note left/right difference), quads, hamstrings, glutes (ankle crossed over opposite knee, lean in).

4 — Couch stretch

If not already done today — 60–90 s each side.

Achilles watch — the non-negotiables

›
✓Traffic light. 0–2/10 soreness fading within the run or a few hours → carry on. 3–4/10, or stiffness lingering >24 h → swap the next quality run for easy or rest, halve calf-eccentric volume. Sharp pain, or morning heel-stiffness worsening week-on-week → stop running, see a physio.
ⓘKeep the eccentrics in. The slow 3-second-down calf raises stay in every lower session, even in the taper (just lighter) — and the daily heel drops continue on every other day.
ⓘUphill yes, downhill careful. Hill reps load the Achilles less than fast downhills — always jog the descents.
△No volume spikes. Week-to-week jumps stay ~10% with a built-in down week (W27). Walk the Thursday commutes on a tired week to shed load.
ⓘShoes. Cushioned Nimbus for easy/long days takes tension off the tendon; EVO SL only for quality. Pegasus stays the backup. Chuck 70s for the gym.

Strength — machines, loads & progression

›

Two sessions a week, placed so they never sabotage a run: upper + core on Monday (legs fresh for Tuesday quality), lower + calves on Wednesday (day after quality, four days clear of the long run). Each day tile shows that week's exact loads. New machines arrive at no more than two per session.

Loading rules — written for 7 kg pin jumps ›
  • 2 reps in reserve on every working set. First session on any machine is for finding the working weight, not chasing it.
  • Reps before load. The selectorised stacks move in verified 7 kg steps — enormous jumps at starting loads (7→14 kg doubles the shoulder press). So: progress 3×8 → 3×10 → 3×12 at a pin. When 3×12 feels like 2-in-reserve two sessions running, go up one pin and drop back to 3×8.
  • DAP is the fine-grained tool — 2.5 kg per-side steps (max 40 kg/side). Cable exercises can progress by load normally.
  • OSSP is counter-balanced — actual starting resistance is lighter than the pin weight. If 7 kg feels unloaded, work up pin by pin in the first session until it's a true 2-in-reserve 10.
  • Dumbbell movements (pullover, RDL, BSS) progress to the next dumbbell up when ready; the 10→20 kg gap is why these migrate to machines over time.

Race day — Sun 9 Aug · Hyde Park → Bondi

›
⚠Rule: nothing new on race day. No new shoes, no new gel, no unfamiliar breakfast.

Pre-race

  1. Normal, familiar breakfast 2–3 h before the start
  2. Sip water through the morning — don't chug in the last 30 min
  3. Gel in the shorts pocket; no bottle needed (stations every 2–3 km)

On course

  1. ~3 km station: 1–2 small sips
  2. ~6 km station: 2–3 sips — take the gel at 45–50 min or the 6–7 km mark, whichever comes first. Heartbreak Hill starts ~8.5 km and absorption takes 10–15 min: fuel must be on board before the hill.
  3. Post-hill stations: ongoing small sips into the finish — controlled, not gulping, to cut stitch risk

Heartbreak Hill

Resist attacking it. Shorten the stride, keep cadence up, stay controlled — crest with enough left to run the back section home. Time is made on the descent and flat after the hill, not on the way up. Then let the final drop into Bondi run.

Daily nutrition — corrected for 58 kg

›
✓Protein: 105–130 g/day (1.8–2.2 g/kg at 58 kg). Supports muscle repair and tendon collagen synthesis — tendons adapt slowly and need it daily. Three meals each with ~25–35 g of quality protein (chicken, fish, eggs, Greek yoghurt, legumes) plus incidentals gets there. The previous guide assumed 75–80 kg — its 135–175 g target was an error for you.
ⓘCarbohydrates: don't undereat them, especially on quality days — they're the running fuel. No benefit to restricting during a training block.

Pre-run

  1. Easy runs, fasted morning: fine as-is, no food needed
  2. Quality sessions: small snack 60–90 min before — banana or toast

Post-run — within 30–60 min

  1. 20–40 g protein + carbs — milk and fruit, or Greek yoghurt and banana, is enough
  2. Matters most after quality and long runs
△Sleep: 7–9 h, consistent times — adaptation happens during sleep, not during the runs. Hard sessions after 7 pm can delay sleep onset; easy treadmill at 8 pm is fine, intervals or tempo late are worth avoiding.

Body composition note: at 58 kg and early-stage strength training, solid gains are achievable near maintenance calories — but only if the protein target is met consistently. While City2Surf is the priority: fuel for running first.
